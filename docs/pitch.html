WHAT CHANGED

The same shop, easier to read

The catalogue and photos are the ones you already have. What changed is the presentation:

  • One language throughout. Pages that mixed Dutch and English now read as a single English site, which is where most of your customers are.
  • Your OEM work reads as work. Projects for Brompton, Hase, ICE and others show an enquiry button instead of a price, so nobody tries to put a client project in a shopping cart.
  • Pages are light and quick, on a phone as much as on a desktop.
  • Every page carries a proper description for search engines, and the site ships with a full sitemap, so people searching for a trailer can actually find you.
DAY TO DAY

You can run it yourselves

Most website work at a company your size is routine: a price changes, a product sells out, a new photo replaces an old one. You should be able to do all of that in-house, without waiting on anyone.

The good news is that the system behind your current shop already supports this, and it also supports the things you mentioned wanting: shipping prices that differ per destination, and payment by iDEAL, card or bank transfer. Putting this design live on what you already own keeps the routine work in your hands.

I wrote the day-to-day out screen by screen, along with a fixed-price job list for the one-time install: the store owner's kit. It is useful whether or not you use the rest of this.
