01

Who does what

Almost everything a shop like yours needs done month to month -- change a shipping price, add a product, edit a page, turn on a payment method -- is built into Craft Commerce and meant to be done by the owner, from the admin screen, with no code.

The work that genuinely needs a developer is one-time (installing the new design) or occasional (security updates a few times a year). Both can be bought as fixed jobs or a small hourly arrangement.

You do yourselves
The routine

Shipping edits, product changes, price updates, content, payment settings, order handling -- all done from the control panel in minutes.

You pay for
Hosting + hours

Web hosting (you already pay this), and a handful of maintenance hours a year for updates.

03

Shipping by country

Craft Commerce charges different shipping prices to different countries out of the box. You build zones (groups of countries) and rules (the price for each zone, which can vary by weight). No plugin, no code -- it only needs to be set up once.

Set it up once

  1. Open the shipping screen. Commerce → Shipping (wording varies slightly by version). You'll see Shipping Zones and Shipping Methods.
  2. Create your zones. Add a zone per group of countries -- e.g. "Zone 1 -- Netherlands", "Zone 2 -- BE/DE/FR", up to "Zone 5 -- Rest of world". Tick the countries in each.
  3. Add a shipping method called "Standard shipping" (and optionally "Express").
  4. Add one rule per zone under that method. Set the price. Because the trailer is bulky, use a per-order base rate rather than per-kilo.
  5. Add a free "Workshop pickup" method with a single rule priced at 0. Customers who pick this collect in Gasselternijveen.

04

Stripe, iDEAL, and bank transfer

Three payment methods cover almost every customer you have: card and iDEAL through Stripe, and manual bank transfer for pickup orders. All three live under Commerce → Settings → Gateways.

Stripe -- cards and iDEAL

Stripe is a first-party option for Craft Commerce. One connection gives you Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and iDEAL (the way most Dutch customers pay). The developer installs the Stripe plugin once; after that you manage it yourselves.

  1. Make a free Stripe account at stripe.com and finish the business verification (bank details, KvK number).
  2. Copy your two API keys from the Stripe dashboard (a publishable key and a secret key). Stripe → Developers → API keys
  3. Paste them into the Stripe gateway in Craft. Commerce → Settings → Gateways → Stripe Turn on card and iDEAL.
  4. Test with Stripe's test card (4242 4242 4242 4242), then switch the keys from test to live.

What Stripe costs

No monthly fee. Stripe takes a small percentage per sale (roughly 1.5% + €0.25 on European cards; iDEAL is a few cents flat). You keep the rest. Payouts land in your bank automatically.

Bank transfer -- for pickups and big orders

Craft Commerce has a built-in Manual gateway for offline payment. This is the flow you already use: the customer places the order, it sits as "pending", you send an invoice with your IBAN and a payment link, and you release the trailer once the money arrives. Nothing to install -- just switch it on.

  1. Add a "Manual / bank transfer" gateway. Commerce → Settings → Gateways → New → Manual
  2. Name it "Bank transfer (invoice)" so customers recognise it at checkout.
  3. That's it. Orders paid this way arrive marked as pending payment for you to confirm by hand.

The pickup charge, handled cleanly

When a customer chooses workshop pickup, the free "Pickup" shipping method (section 03) already sets shipping to zero -- so there is no delivery line to strike off the invoice by hand. If an order slips through with shipping on it, you can still edit the order and remove the charge before sending the payment link.

05

Handling an order, start to finish

Every order lands under Commerce → Orders. Here is the pickup + bank-transfer case, which is your most common one.

  1. Order comes in as "pending payment" with the customer's note (pickup date, hitch details).
  2. Open it, check the note, and confirm the axle/fit question if they asked one -- before anything is drilled.
  3. Send the invoice + payment link. Craft can email a link; the customer pays by card or transfer.
  4. Mark it paid once the money clears (automatic for card/iDEAL, by hand for a transfer).
  5. Prep the trailer and confirm the collection slot. Mark the order complete.
  • Refund: open the order, click Refund -- for Stripe payments the money returns to the card automatically.
  • 14-day withdrawal: online orders carry a 14-day right of withdrawal; the refund button covers it.
  • VAT: set once under Commerce → Tax (21% for NL). Prices show correctly and appear on every invoice.
06

Products, prices, and stock

Products live under Commerce → Products. Adding or changing one is a form, not a code change.

  • New product: click New, fill in the name, price, photos and description, hit Save.
  • Options (like the M10 vs M12 hitch, or wheel size): add them as "variants" -- each can have its own price and stock count.
  • Add-ons (spare hitch ball, walking towbar, carry system): separate products the customer can add to the cart.
  • Stock: set a number and Craft hides "add to cart" when it hits zero, or set it to unlimited.
  • Price change: open the product, change the number, Save. Live immediately.
07

Pages, photos, and the homepage

Text and images across the site are "entries" in Craft, editable in a normal editor -- headings, paragraphs, photos, links. You do not need a developer to fix a typo, swap a photo, update opening hours, or write a news post. If the new design (section 08) is built with editable regions, even the homepage banner and product blurbs are yours to change.

Ask the installer for this

When you hire someone for the one-time install, tell them: "make the homepage sections, product text and images editable from the control panel." A good build makes almost everything owner-editable -- that's what keeps the routine work in your hands.

Hiring for the install -- at a fixed price

The point of a fixed scope is that any competent Craft freelancer can quote it as a job with a number and an end date. Send the message below to two or three Craft developers (the Craft community keeps a directory of them) and compare quotes.

Request for quote -- paste and send
Subject: Fixed-price Craft Commerce job -- new theme + payment/shipping setup

Hello,

We run radicaldesign.com on Craft CMS and Craft Commerce and want a fixed-price
quote for a one-time job:

1. Build a new front-end theme from a complete, finished static rebuild of the
   site we will provide (every page, HTML/CSS, no framework dependencies).
   Convert it to Twig templates wired to our real products: homepage, listing,
   product detail, cart, checkout, order confirmation, and the standard content
   pages. Make homepage sections, product text and images editable from the
   control panel.

2. Install and connect the Stripe gateway (cards + iDEAL) and the built-in
   Manual gateway for bank transfer. We will supply the Stripe keys.

3. Confirm our zone-based shipping and VAT are configured (we can also do this
   ourselves -- please leave it owner-editable).

4. Optional line item: install the Postie plugin for live carrier rates -- quote
   separately.

Please send: a fixed price, a timeline, and what you would need from us to
start. We maintain the site ourselves after handover, so no ongoing contract is
required -- though we may want a small hourly rate for occasional updates.

Thank you,
Radical Design

Why a fixed scope works

A fixed scope with a finished design is quotable and comparable. You are buying a defined result, once, from whoever gives the best price. After handover, the site is yours to run.

10

Go-live checklist

Work top to bottom. The first block is yours; the install block is the freelancer's.

You (in the control panel)

  • Shipping zones created (Zones 1--5 + free Pickup)
  • A shipping rule and price set for every zone
  • VAT set to 21% under Commerce → Tax
  • All current products, prices and stock entered
  • Opening hours, contact and pickup address up to date

The install (freelancer)

  • New theme built from the provided pages and live
  • Stripe plugin installed; card + iDEAL tested with a real €1 order
  • Manual bank-transfer gateway switched on
  • Homepage, product text and images left owner-editable
  • Order confirmation email sends correctly
  • A full test order placed, paid, and refunded end to end
11

What still needs a developer -- honestly

So you go in clear-eyed: a self-hosted Craft site is not zero-maintenance. A few things sit outside the control panel.

  • Security updates: Craft and its plugins release updates through the year. Skipping them is a real risk. This is a small, occasional job -- an hour here and there.
  • Hosting: the site runs on a server you pay for regardless. You already have this.
  • The odd bug or new feature: anything genuinely new to the code needs a developer's hands.

Two ways to cover that

Managed hosting -- a host that handles Craft updates as part of the hosting fee, a predictable monthly cost. Or hourly on-call -- keep a freelancer's rate on file and pay only for the hours you use.
